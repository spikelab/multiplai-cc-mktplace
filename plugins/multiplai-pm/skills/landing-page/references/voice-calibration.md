# Landing Page Voice Calibration

Voice overlay for landing page copy. This file defines what's inherited, what's overridden, and what's added compared to the user's core voice.

---

## How This Overlay Works

Landing page copy is fundamentally different from blog posts or professional writing. It's persuasive, concise, and action-oriented. The user's core voice (core-voice.md) is designed for reflective, peer-to-peer writing — several of its rules explicitly ban marketing patterns.

This overlay:
1. **Inherits** the principles that make writing good regardless of context
2. **Overrides** the anti-marketing rules that would cripple landing page copy
3. **Adds** landing page-specific voice patterns

When writing landing page copy, this overlay takes precedence over core-voice.md for the specific rules listed below. For anything not explicitly overridden here, core-voice.md remains the authority.

---

## Inherited from Core Voice (These Still Apply)

### Always Active

- **Clarity over cleverness** — even more important on landing pages than in blog posts
- **Specificity** — "4,200 teams" not "thousands of users"
- **No buzzwords** — seamless, revolutionary, game-changing, best-in-class, and the full banned list from core-voice.md remain banned
- **No hype** — "ground-breaking", "ultimate", "crushing it" are still out
- **No LinkedIn copywriting patterns** — manufactured hooks, self-congratulatory bridges, clever punchlines
- **Active voice** — "We built X" not "X was built"
- **Contractions** — "You'll", "We've", "It's" — keeps copy conversational
- **Spelling** — match your brand's convention consistently; if your brand uses British spelling, prefer colour, realise, behaviour, organisation
- **No AI vocabulary** — crucial, pivotal, robust, comprehensive, dynamic, leveraging, fostering, delving, tapestry, testament, interplay
- **Em dash limit** — max 2-3 per page. Default to commas, colons, or full stops.

### Write-Like-a-Human Rules (All Active)

The full AI-tell avoidance checklist from write-like-a-human.md applies to landing page copy:
- No generic praise words without concrete examples nearby
- No over-structured triplets and parallel constructions
- No formulaic section names ("Introduction", "Conclusion")
- No "helpful assistant" tone
- Vary sentence length and structure
- No elegant variation (don't swap synonyms to avoid repetition)

## Overridden for Landing Pages

These core-voice rules are suspended when writing landing page copy:

### Danger Zone 4: Marketing-Style CTAs → OVERRIDDEN

**Core-voice says:** "If you end with 'Sign up now!', or adopt a high-energy sales tone, the voice breaks."
**Landing page override:** CTAs are not just allowed — they're essential. But they must follow the CTA formula: **[Action Verb] + [What They Get] + [Qualifier]**.

| Banned (generic/pushy) | Allowed (specific/valuable) |
|------------------------|-----------------------------|
| "Sign up now!" | "Start your free trial" |
| "Buy today!" | "Get the complete toolkit" |
| "Don't miss out!" | "Join 4,200 teams" |
| "Submit" | "See pricing for my team" |
| "Click here" | "Download the guide" |

**The line:** CTAs that describe what the visitor gets = good. CTAs that pressure or command = still banned.

### Red Line: "Salesy" Language → PARTIALLY OVERRIDDEN

**Core-voice says:** "No pushy marketing tone ('buy this', 'you must')."
**Landing page override:** Persuasive copy is allowed when it's grounded in specifics and outcomes. What's still banned is empty persuasion.

| Still banned | Now allowed |
|-------------|------------|
| "You MUST try this" | "See how 340 teams cut invoice time by 80%" |
| "Don't miss this opportunity" | "Join the founding group — 47 spots left" (only if real) |
| "The best tool ever made" | "The fastest invoice tool for solo consultants" |
| Empty superlatives | Specific, verifiable claims |

### Danger Zone 3: Listicle Format → PARTIALLY OVERRIDDEN

**Core-voice says:** "A purely bulleted list without story and reflection lacks the voice's layered structure."
**Landing page override:** Benefit blocks, feature lists, and social proof sections ARE structured lists by nature. They don't need narrative framing. But each item must have substance — no empty bullets.

### Story → Insight → Application Structure → NOT REQUIRED

**Core-voice says:** Every piece should flow from premise → grounding → reflection → takeaway.
**Landing page override:** Landing pages follow the buyer's decision flow (What is this → Can I trust it → How does it help → What do I do). Stories are optional — used in testimonials and founder sections, not as the page's structural backbone.

## Added for Landing Pages

### Concision

Landing page copy is radically shorter than blog or professional writing:
- **Headlines:** 5-12 words
- **Subheadlines:** 10-25 words
- **Benefit descriptions:** 2-3 sentences max
- **CTA microcopy:** 3-8 words
- **FAQ answers:** 2-3 sentences each

Every word must earn its place. If a sentence doesn't increase desire, build confidence, reduce effort, or reduce confusion — cut it.

### Second-Person Dominance

Landing pages speak directly to the visitor:
- "You" is the dominant pronoun (not "I" or "we")
- "Your team", "your workflow", "your customers"
- The company speaks in "we" only when establishing credibility ("We built X because...")
- First-person ("I") appears only in testimonial quotes

This is a significant departure from core-voice, which prefers first-person reflection. On landing pages, the visitor is the protagonist.

### Urgency and Scarcity (Constrained)

**Allowed when real:**
- "Early access — founding member pricing ends March 31" (real deadline)
- "47 spots remaining in the beta" (real limit)
- "Free during beta. Paid plans start Q2." (real timeline)

**Never allowed:**
- Fake countdown timers
- Manufactured scarcity ("Only 3 left!" when there's no limit)
- Pressure tactics ("Buy now before it's too late!")
- False deadlines

**Rule:** If you can't verify the scarcity is real, don't use it. Authenticity is a trust signal.

### Social Proof Voice

Testimonials and case studies have their own voice rules:
- Quote real language — don't polish customer quotes into marketing-speak
- Include specifics: company name, role, metrics, timeframe
- Minor imperfections in quotes are fine (increase credibility)
- Attribution must be verifiable: full name + role + company, or link to public profile

### Brand Personality Axes

Set during discovery. These calibrate the copy tone:

| Axis | Range | Affects |
|------|-------|---------|
| **Formality** | casual ↔ formal | Pronoun use, sentence structure, vocabulary level |
| **Energy** | calm ↔ energetic | Exclamation marks (rare even at energetic), verb choice, rhythm |
| **Technical** | accessible ↔ deep | How much mechanism you explain vs. just outcomes |
| **Warmth** | professional ↔ personal | Use of "I/we" vs. company name, founder story inclusion |

**A common starting default** (adjust to your brand): casual, calm-to-moderate energy, accessible with technical depth available, personal.

## Voice Self-Test for Landing Pages

After writing copy, check:

1. **5-second test:** Can someone read just the hero and explain what this product does?
2. **"So what?" test:** For each benefit, would the visitor say "so what?" or "that's exactly what I need"?
3. **Buzzword scan:** Search for the banned word list. Replace every hit with specifics.
4. **AI-tell scan:** Would this copy sit in a LinkedIn carousel? Would an AI generate this exact phrasing? If yes, rewrite.
5. **Customer voice test:** Does the copy use words the customer would use, or words the product team uses?
6. **Authenticity check:** Is every claim verifiable? Is every testimonial real? Is every number accurate?
7. **Single-CTA test:** Is there one clear action? Or is the visitor choosing between multiple asks?
