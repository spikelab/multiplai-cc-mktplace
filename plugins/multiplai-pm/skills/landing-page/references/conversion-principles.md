# Conversion Principles Reference

Evidence-based landing page conversion principles. Load this file when writing or auditing landing page copy.

---

## The Conversion Formula

**Purchase Rate = (Desire + Confidence) / (Effort + Confusion)**

Every element on the page either increases the numerator or decreases the denominator. When reviewing copy, ask for each section: which lever does this pull?

| Lever | What increases it | What decreases it |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Desire** | Benefit-driven copy, outcome language, loss aversion framing | Feature lists, jargon, talking about the company |
| **Confidence** | Social proof, specifics, credibility signals, demos | Vague claims, no proof, stock photos, buzzwords |
| **Effort** (lower = better) | Clear CTA, minimal forms, fast page | Multiple CTAs, long forms, slow load, many steps |
| **Confusion** (lower = better) | One idea per section, clear hierarchy, plain language | Multiple messages, cluttered layout, jargon, inconsistent messaging |

Test and optimize in this order (highest measured impact first):

| Priority | Optimization | Measured Lift | What to do |
|----------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| 1 | Form field reduction | 120% (11→4 fields) | Cut every field that isn't strictly necessary |
| 2 | Headline copy | 27-104% | Test benefit-driven vs problem-driven vs specific outcome |

## Five Copy Principles

### 1. Clarity Over Cleverness

If a visitor cannot understand what you offer within 5 seconds, nothing else matters. A confused person bounces.

**Test:** Cover everything below the hero. Can someone read just the headline and subheadline and explain what this product does? If not, rewrite.

**Bad:** "Unlock the Power of Intelligent Automation"
**Good:** "Send invoices in 30 seconds. Get paid 2x faster."

### 2. Benefits Over Features

Nobody wakes up wanting a "robust API integration." They want to save time, make money, or stop a pain.

**Transform features into outcomes:**
- Feature: "AI-powered analytics" → Benefit: "Know which customers will churn before they leave"
- Feature: "Real-time sync" → Benefit: "Your whole team sees the same data, always"
- Feature: "Save time" → Specific benefit: "4 hours to 15 minutes"

### 3. Specificity Over Vagueness

Generic value props say nothing. Approximately 95% of landing pages are buzzword-stuffed.

**Banned words** (flag these during audit): seamless, revolutionary, game-changing, next-generation, cutting-edge, best-in-class, world-class, AI-led, robust, comprehensive, innovative, disruptive, synergy, leverage, holistic, empower, transform, elevate, solutions for growth, results you can trust.

**Replace with specifics:**
- "Fast results" → "See your first report in under 2 minutes"
- "Trusted by thousands" → "Used by 4,200 teams across 38 countries"
- "Affordable pricing" → "Starts at $29/month. No setup fees."

### 4. Customer Language Over Insider Jargon

Mirror the exact words customers use in reviews, support tickets, and interviews. Voice-of-customer research applied to copy.

**Process:** If the user has customer feedback, reviews, or support tickets, extract the language they use to describe their problem and desired outcome. Use those exact phrases in headlines and body copy.

### 5. One Idea Per Section

Each page section advances a single argument. Multiple messages in one section create cognitive overload. If you're saying two things, split into two sections.

## CTA Formulas

### Button Copy Formula

**[Action Verb] + [What They Get] + [Qualifier]**

| Strong | Weak | Why |
|--------|------|-----|
| "Start My Free Trial" | "Submit" | Describes the outcome, not the action |
| "Get the Complete Checklist" | "Learn More" | Specific about what they receive |
| "See Pricing for My Team" | "Click Here" | Relevant to their intent |
| "Download the Guide" | "Get Started" | Clear deliverable |

### CTA Microcopy

Supporting text below/near the button that overcomes hesitation:
- "No credit card required"
- "Takes 30 seconds"
- "Free for teams under 5"
- "Cancel anytime"
- "Join 4,200 other teams"

### Key Insight

The word "trial" outperforms "sign up" by 104% because it implies temporary evaluation, not permanent commitment. Frame CTAs around what the visitor gets to try, not what they're committing to.

## Headline Formulas

Tested patterns that work across contexts:

| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| {Achieve outcome} without {pain point} | "Grow your email list without writing a single blog post" |
| The {category} for {audience} | "The CRM for solo consultants" |
| Never {unpleasant event} again | "Never miss a customer message again" |
| {Question highlighting main pain point}? | "Still spending 4 hours a week on invoices?" |
| {Specific number} {audience} use {product} to {outcome} | "2,400 startups use Acme to close deals 40% faster" |
| Stop {pain}. Start {desired state}. | "Stop guessing. Start knowing what your customers want." |

## Social Proof

### Types (in order of impact)

1. **Customer logos** — recognisable brands below the hero (260% lift for DocSend)
2. **Testimonials with quotes** — outperform logos alone by 35% (Mutiny A/B test)
3. **Specific metrics** — "4,200 teams", "99.9% uptime", "$2.3M saved"
4. **Case studies** — detailed transformation stories
5. **Expert endorsements** — industry authorities
6. **Media mentions** — "As Seen In" with publication logos
7. **Awards and certifications** — trust badges
8. **User-generated content** — screenshots, social posts

### Placement Strategy

Sprinkle throughout — don't concentrate in one section:
- Trust logos just below the hero
- Testimonial quotes near headlines
- Specific stats adjacent to CTAs
- Case study links near benefits sections

### Psychology Notes

- **90% of buyers** say social proof influences their decisions (Gartner)
- Purchase likelihood **peaks at 4.0-4.7 star ratings**, then decreases — perfect 5-star triggers scepticism
- Minor critiques in testimonials **increase** credibility vs. perfect reviews
- **Testimonials from "people like them"** convert best for SMBs — match the testimonial persona to the visitor persona

## Loss Aversion Framing

Pain of losing feels ~2x stronger than pleasure of equivalent gain. Reframe benefits as loss prevention when appropriate:

| Gain Frame (weaker) | Loss Frame (stronger) |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| "Gain 20% more customers" | "Stop losing customers to competitors" |
| "Save 4 hours per week" | "You're wasting 4 hours every week on manual work" |
| "Improve team productivity" | "Your team is losing $50K/year to inefficient tools" |

Use loss framing selectively — it's powerful but overuse feels manipulative. Best for the hero section or final CTA, not every section.

## Cognitive Fluency

- **Reading level:** 8th grade maximum. Simple words, short sentences.
- **Sentence length:** 15-20 words max on average.
- **Active voice:** "We built X" not "X was built by our team."
- **One idea per sentence.** If a sentence needs "and" or "but" to connect two ideas, consider splitting.

## Common Conversion Killers

### 1. Multiple Competing CTAs
Pages with a single link convert at 13.5%, dropping to 10.5% with 5+ links. Around 48% of landing pages still make this mistake. **One primary CTA, repeated. No competing asks.**

### 2. Message Mismatch (Ad → Page)
When ad copy promises something specific but the landing page says something different, trust collapses. **The headline should echo the ad that brought the visitor.**

### 3. Premature Form Placement
Forms at the top of the page create friction before visitors understand the value proposition. **CTA buttons above the fold; multi-field forms below, after value is established.**

### 4. Missing FAQ Section
17% of all clicks on analysed landing pages were in the FAQ section. Visitors actively seek objection-handling content. **Always include FAQ. Use it to answer the objections you'd address in a sales call.**

### 5. Writing for the Company, Not the Customer
"We provide enterprise-grade solutions for..." — nobody cares about you. They care about themselves. **Every sentence should answer: "What does this mean for me?"**

### 6. Navigation That Leaks
Every nav link is an exit. Landing pages should have minimal or no navigation. **Remove the full site nav. Keep only a logo (linking home) and the CTA.**

### 7. Mobile Neglect
Mobile-desktop conversion gap is 40-51%. Desktop averages 4.8-5.06%, mobile 2.49-2.9%. **Test on mobile first. Every section must work on a phone screen.**
