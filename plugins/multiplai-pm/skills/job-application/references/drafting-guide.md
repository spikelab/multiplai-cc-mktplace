# Drafting Guide

## Authenticity Constraints (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

### What You CAN Do
- Reword, restructure, reorder, compress existing content
- Emphasize achievements that align with the JD
- Use stronger action verbs for existing accomplishments
- Mirror JD language where it accurately describes existing experience
- Remove irrelevant experience to keep CV concise

### What You CANNOT Do
- Invent achievements, metrics, technologies, or responsibilities
- Add experience not in career-history.md
- Fabricate quantitative results (percentages, dollar amounts, team sizes)
- Present goals/targets as achieved results
- Claim expertise in technologies not mentioned

### Goals vs. Actuals
If career-history says "targeting X%" or "aiming for Y":
- This is a goal, NOT an achievement
- Reframe as initiative scope: "Led initiative to improve X" (no number)
- Never present goals as achieved results

### Priority Order (When Goals Conflict)
1. Factual accuracy — Never fabricate or embellish
2. Keyword alignment — Mirror JD language where it fits truthfully
3. Conciseness — Keep CV to one page
4. Comprehensiveness — Include relevant experience

If keyword matching would require stretching the truth, skip that keyword.

```
# [YOUR_NAME_UPPER] / [Short Title - must fit on one line]

---

[2-3 line summary: Identity + Scope + Proof points with numbers]

**Core Competencies:** [8-10 JD-matched terms on single line, pipe-separated]

---

[3-4 most relevant roles, 2-3 bullets each]

---

## Education & Certifications
[Use table format with Education and Certificates columns]
```

## Cover Letter Structure (350 words max)

1. **Hook** — Their problem + your fit in one sentence
   - **If applying Senior PM with Director title:** Use "selective, not desperate" opening (see ats-checks.md positioning strategy)
2. **Evidence** — 1-2 concrete examples with metrics
3. **Why This Company** — If context available
4. **Close** — Clear call to action, no begging

**CRITICAL CHECK:** Read opening aloud and ask "Would I hire this person?" If no → rewrite to lead with strength.

## AI-Tell Detection (Run Before Saving)

### Kill List (search and rewrite)
- "crucial", "pivotal", "plays a vital role"
- "serves as a testament", "underscores", "interplay"
- "fostering", "enhancing", "showcasing"
- "in today's rapidly evolving landscape"
- "leveraging the power of", "driving meaningful impact"
- "I am excited to apply", "I believe my passion"
- "incredible opportunity", "dynamic and results-oriented"

### Human Counter-Moves
- Replace abstract praise with specific description
- Break triplets into 2 or 4 items
- Mix sentence lengths intentionally
- Anchor every claim in a specific example from career-history.md

## Internal Truth ≠ External Positioning

**NEVER confuse honest self-assessment with resume/cover letter messaging.**

Examples of this failure:
- Internal: "I lack reps at good companies" → External: "I'm seeking experience to learn" (SUICIDE)
- Internal: "I'm weak at stakeholder politics" → External: "I struggle with convincing people" (SUICIDE)

Correct external framing:
- Internal: "I lack reps at good companies" → External: "I'm selective about where I work—looking for product excellence"
- Internal: "I'm weak at stakeholder politics" → External: "I'm better at building than politicking—prefer hands-on work"
- Internal: "I haven't shipped anything" → External: "I've built platforms and led teams—want to work where good work ships"

**Mandatory check before saving:**
1. Read the cover letter opening aloud
2. Ask: "Would this make ME want to hire this person?"
3. If "no" or "maybe" → REWRITE to lead with strength
4. Never give hiring manager reasons to reject you
